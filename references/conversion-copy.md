# SaaS Conversion Copy Reference

This is the authoritative reference for writing SaaS landing page copy that converts. Every rule here is derived from tested patterns across high-performing SaaS pages. Follow this document exactly when generating copy for any section of a landing page.

---

## 1. The Conversion Sequence

Every SaaS landing page follows this exact sequence. Each section serves one job and one job only. Do not reorder. Do not skip sections. Do not combine jobs.

### Section 1: STOP THE SCROLL (Hero)

**Job**: The visitor decides to stay or leave in 5 seconds.

- Headline must communicate the single biggest outcome the product delivers.
- Subheadline expands with specifics (how it works, what you get, in what timeframe).
- One primary CTA above the fold. One optional secondary CTA.
- Hero image or demo screenshot shows the product in action, not abstract graphics.
- No navigation clutter. Minimal links. The eye goes: headline, subheadline, CTA.

### Section 2: BUILD TRUST (Social Proof Bar)

**Job**: The visitor decides you are legitimate.

- Logo bar of recognizable customers or press mentions.
- Appears immediately below the hero, before any other content.
- 4-6 logos maximum. Grayscale. No hover effects that distract.
- If no logos yet: use a metrics bar ("2,847 teams use [Product]" or "Rated 4.9/5 on G2").

### Section 3: AGITATE THE PAIN (Problem)

**Job**: The visitor feels understood.

- Describe the painful status quo in the customer's own words.
- Use "you" language. Never "users" or "customers" in copy aimed at the reader.
- Name the specific frustrations: wasted time, lost revenue, manual work, broken workflows.
- Do not mention your product in this section. This section is about their world.
- End with a transition sentence that opens the door to a solution.

### Section 4: SHOW THE SOLUTION (Features)

**Job**: The visitor sees exactly how you solve their problem.

- 3-5 features maximum. Each with a benefit clause (see Section 4 below).
- Visual proof for each feature: screenshot, short GIF, or diagram.
- Features map 1:1 to the pains described in Section 3.
- Use alternating layout (image-left/text-right, then flip) for visual rhythm.

### Section 5: PROVE IT WORKS (Testimonials)

**Job**: The visitor believes the solution actually delivers.

- 2-3 testimonials from named people with titles, companies, and photos.
- Each testimonial includes a specific result or outcome, not vague praise.
- Place testimonials near pricing or after features, never buried at the bottom.
- Pull quotes that address the most common objection for your audience.

### Section 6: REMOVE DOUBT (Pricing + FAQ)

**Job**: The visitor's remaining objections are answered.

- Pricing: 2-3 tiers. Highlight the recommended tier. Show annual savings.
- FAQ: Address the 5-7 most common objections (see Section 7 below).
- Keep pricing simple. If it takes more than 10 seconds to understand, simplify.
- Trust badges near the pricing CTA: security certs, uptime guarantees, review scores.

### Section 7: MAKE THE ASK (Final CTA)

**Job**: The visitor converts.

- Full-width CTA section with headline restating the core outcome.
- Repeat the primary CTA from the hero. Same text. Same color.
- Add urgency only if genuine (limited beta spots, launch pricing deadline).
- Trust hint below CTA: "Free 14-day trial. No credit card required. Cancel anytime."
- This is the last thing on the page before the footer. Make it count.

## 2. Headline Formulas by Awareness Level

Before writing a headline, determine the visitor's awareness level. The wrong headline for the awareness level kills conversion instantly.

### Problem-Aware Visitors

They know they have a problem but have not started looking for solutions yet.

**Formula**: `[Pain verb] + [specific pain] + [for whom]`

Lead with their pain. Make them feel seen.

**Examples**:
- "Stop Losing Deals Because Your Team Can't Find the Right Document"
- "Tired of Manually Updating 14 Spreadsheets Every Monday Morning?"
- "Your Engineers Spend 30% of Their Time on Deployment. That's Fixable."
- "Still Chasing Invoices 60 Days After Sending Them?"
- "Drowning in Support Tickets That Could Answer Themselves?"

### Solution-Aware Visitors

They know solutions exist and are comparing categories. They searched "best project management tool" or "CRM for startups."

**Formula**: `The [adjective] [category] for [audience] who [specific need]`

Lead with the category so they know they are in the right place, then differentiate.

**Examples**:
- "The Fastest CI/CD Pipeline for Teams Shipping Multiple Times a Day"
- "The CRM Built for Founders Who Sell, Not Sales Ops Who Configure"
- "Email Marketing That Feels Personal at 100,000 Subscribers"
- "The Support Platform for SaaS Teams Who Measure Resolution Time, Not Ticket Count"
- "Project Management for Remote Teams Who Actually Ship on Deadline"

### Product-Aware Visitors

They know your product. They came from a referral, a review site, or a retargeting ad. They need one reason to act now.

**Formula**: `[Specific unique capability] + [so that outcome]`

Lead with what makes you different from the alternatives they already know.

**Examples**:
- "One-Click Deploys to 14 Cloud Providers -- Ship Today, Not Next Sprint"
- "AI Writes Your Changelog From Git Commits -- So Your Team Stops Dreading Release Notes"
- "Connect Your Entire Stack in 90 Seconds With Pre-Built Integrations"
- "See Every Customer Interaction on One Screen -- No More Tab-Switching During Calls"
- "Automated SOC 2 Evidence Collection -- Pass Your Audit 3x Faster"

## 3. CTA Copy Rules

These rules are backed by A/B test data across SaaS landing pages. Do not deviate.

### High-Performing CTA Text

| CTA Text | Best For | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| "Start Free Trial" | Product-focused traffic, solution-aware visitors | Action-oriented, removes cost objection upfront |
| "Trial for Free" | General traffic | Converts 104% better than "Sign up for free" (tested) |
| "See How It Works" | Problem-aware traffic, complex products | Low commitment, curiosity-driven |
| "Book a Demo" | Enterprise, high-ticket ($500+/mo), multi-stakeholder | Matches buying behavior for large purchases |
| "Get [Specific Outcome]" | Lead magnets, gated content | Outcome-focused: "Get Your Free Audit Report" |
| "Start [Doing Thing] Free" | Action-oriented audiences | "Start Deploying Free", "Start Tracking Free" |

### CTA Placement Rules

- The primary CTA appears minimum **4 times** on every landing page: hero, after features, after testimonials, final CTA section.
- CTA must be the **highest-contrast element** on every viewport. If a visitor screenshots any section, the CTA should be the first thing they notice.
- Use one primary CTA style (filled, bold color) and one secondary CTA style (outlined or text link) maximum. Never three styles.
- Mobile: CTA must be thumb-reachable. Sticky CTA bar on scroll for long pages.

### Banned CTA Text (Never Use These)

- "Learn More" -- vague, passive, no commitment signal
- "Get Started" -- ambiguous, does not tell the visitor what happens next
- "Submit" -- feels like paperwork
- "Click Here" -- meaningless, inaccessible, amateur
- "Sign Up" -- focuses on the action you want, not the value they get
- "Download" -- unless it is literally a file download
- "Join Now" -- vague, no outcome communicated

### CTA Trust Hints

Always place a trust hint directly below or beside the CTA button:

```
[  Start Free Trial  ]
No credit card required. Cancel anytime.
```

```
[  Book a Demo  ]
30-minute call. No commitment. See it live.
```

```
[  Get Your Free Report  ]
Takes 2 minutes. No email spam.
```

## 4. Feature to Benefit Transformation

Every feature on the page MUST include a "so that" benefit clause. Features without benefits are meaningless to visitors who do not already understand your product.

### The Rule

```
[Feature] -- so [specific outcome for the reader]
```

### BAD vs GOOD Examples

**Project Management SaaS**:
- BAD: "Real-time dashboard"
- GOOD: "Real-time dashboard -- so you know exactly what's happening right now, not 24 hours ago"

**Analytics SaaS**:
- BAD: "AI-powered analytics"
- GOOD: "AI spots the patterns you'd miss -- so you fix problems before customers notice"

**Security SaaS**:
- BAD: "Enterprise-grade security"
- GOOD: "SOC 2 compliant -- so your security team signs off in days, not months"

**DevOps SaaS**:
- BAD: "Automated deployments"
- GOOD: "Push to deploy in one click -- so your engineers ship features instead of fighting infrastructure"

**HR SaaS**:
- BAD: "Employee engagement surveys"
- GOOD: "Pulse surveys in 2 minutes -- so you catch burnout before your best people quit"

**Finance SaaS**:
- BAD: "Automated invoicing"
- GOOD: "Invoices go out the second a project closes -- so you get paid 15 days faster on average"

**Customer Support SaaS**:
- BAD: "Knowledge base with search"
- GOOD: "Customers find their own answers in under 30 seconds -- so your team handles the hard problems, not password resets"

**Email Marketing SaaS**:
- BAD: "Advanced segmentation"
- GOOD: "Segment by actual behavior, not just demographics -- so every email feels personally written"

### Quantity Rules

- Landing page: 3-5 features maximum. More features = decision paralysis = lower conversion.
- Each feature gets equal visual weight. Do not bury the best feature at the bottom.
- Order features by pain severity: the feature that solves the biggest pain goes first.

## 5. Anti-AI Writing Rules

SaaS copy must sound like a human who understands the customer's problem, not like a language model producing "professional content." These rules are non-negotiable.

### Banned Words (Complete List)

Never use any of these words in landing page copy:

```
leverage, unlock, revolutionize, seamlessly, cutting-edge, game-changing,
elevate, empower, streamline, robust, innovative, comprehensive, holistic,
transform, supercharge, turbocharge, next-generation, state-of-the-art,
harness, delve, navigate, landscape, paradigm, synergy, optimize (when vague),
drive (as in "drive results"), ensure (when overused), utilize, facilitate,
enhance (when vague), spearhead, foster, bolster, underscore, realm, keen,
meticulous, testament, pivotal, moreover, furthermore, notably, importantly,
in today's fast-paced world, at the end of the day, it goes without saying,
take it to the next level, move the needle, best-in-class, world-class,
mission-critical, scalable (when vague), end-to-end, turnkey, frictionless,
future-proof, bleeding-edge, disruptive, groundbreaking, unparalleled
```

### Required Writing Rules

1. **Specific numbers over vague claims**.
   - BAD: "Saves you tons of time"
   - GOOD: "Saves your team 6 hours every week"

2. **Named outcomes over abstract benefits**.
   - BAD: "Improve your workflow"
   - GOOD: "Close deals 40% faster because proposals go out the same day"

3. **Customer language mirroring**. Use the exact words your customers use to describe their problems. Pull from support tickets, sales calls, review sites, and Reddit threads.
   - BAD: "Facilitates cross-functional collaboration"
   - GOOD: "Your marketing and sales teams finally look at the same numbers"

4. **Conversational tone**. Read every sentence aloud. If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it. If you would not say it to a colleague over coffee, rewrite it.

5. **Short sentences in hero copy**. Maximum 20 words per sentence in the hero section. Subheadlines can go to 25. Body copy can go to 30 but shorter is always better.

6. **Active voice always**. The subject does the action.
   - BAD: "Reports are generated automatically"
   - GOOD: "Your dashboard generates reports automatically"

7. **No filler phrases**. Cut "In order to", "It is important to note that", "As a matter of fact", "When it comes to". Just say the thing.

8. **"You" and "your" over "our" and "we"**. The page is about the visitor, not the company.
   - BAD: "We built a powerful analytics engine"
   - GOOD: "You get answers in seconds, not hours"

### Voice Calibration Step

Before writing any copy, the skill MUST ask the user:

> "How do your customers describe the problem your product solves? Paste 2-3 sentences from a customer email, support ticket, sales call transcript, or review. I will mirror their exact language in the copy."

If the user cannot provide customer language, prompt them with:

> "What's the most common complaint your customers had before using your product? How would they describe it to a coworker?"

Use the exact phrasing, vocabulary level, and emotional tone from their response throughout the page copy. This is the single highest-leverage action for conversion copy.

## 6. Social Proof Copy Rules

Social proof is the most abused and most wasted section on SaaS landing pages. These rules ensure it actually builds trust.

### The Specificity Rule

Vague praise does nothing. Specific results convert.

- BAD: "Great product! Really helped our team."
- GOOD: "We cut our onboarding time from 3 weeks to 4 days. Our new hires are productive on day one." -- Sarah Chen, VP of People, Lattice (120 employees)

### Required Elements for Every Testimonial

Each testimonial MUST include all of these:
- **Full name** (first and last)
- **Job title**
- **Company name**
- **Company logo** (displayed alongside the quote)
- **Specific result or outcome** within the quote text
- **Photo of the person** (headshot, not company logo as avatar)

If any element is missing, the testimonial is incomplete and should not be used. Prompt the user to collect the missing elements.

### Placement Rules

| Proof Type | Where It Goes | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Logo bar (customers/press) | Directly below hero | First trust signal after the pitch |
| Metric bar ("12,847 teams") | Below hero or below logo bar | Quantified credibility |
| Testimonials with results | Near pricing section | Overcomes cost objection with proof of ROI |
| Case study snippets | After features section | Proves the features deliver real outcomes |
| Review badges (G2, Capterra) | Near CTA buttons | Third-party validation at decision point |

### Number Rules

- Always use exact numbers: "12,847 teams" not "thousands of teams"
- Always use specific metrics: "4.9/5 on G2 from 847 reviews" not "highly rated"
- Update numbers regularly. Stale numbers ("10,000+ users" for 2 years) signal a dead product.
- Round numbers feel fake. "10,000 users" is less believable than "10,847 users."

### Startup Alternatives (When You Have No Big Logos)

If the product is early-stage and lacks recognizable customer logos, use these alternatives in priority order:

1. **Founder testimonials with results**: "We switched from [Competitor] and saved 12 hours/week" -- with name, photo, company
2. **Beta user count with specificity**: "437 teams joined the beta in the first 2 weeks"
3. **GitHub stars or open-source metrics**: "4,200 GitHub stars" (for dev tools)
4. **ProductHunt rank**: "#1 Product of the Day -- 1,247 upvotes"
5. **Investor names** (only if recognizable to your audience): "Backed by Y Combinator"
6. **Personal brand of founders**: "Built by the team behind [known product]"
7. **Speed/traction metrics**: "From idea to 1,000 users in 47 days"

## 7. Objection Handling Patterns (FAQ Section)

The FAQ section is not a dumping ground for random questions. It is a structured objection-handling tool. Every FAQ answer follows a framework.

### The 5 Universal SaaS Objections

Every SaaS product faces these five objections. Address all of them.

#### Objection 1: "Is it worth the money?"

**Answer Framework**: ROI calculation.

Show the math. Make the value obvious compared to the cost.

```
Q: Is [Product] worth the price?

A: [Product] costs $X/month. The average team saves Y hours per week,
   which is Z dollars in salary costs alone. Most teams see full ROI
   within [timeframe]. Plus, you can try it free for 14 days to prove
   the value before you pay anything.
```

#### Objection 2: "Is it hard to set up?"

**Answer Framework**: Time-to-value + proof.

Give a specific setup time and show it.

```
Q: How long does setup take?

A: Most teams are up and running in under 5 minutes. Connect your
   [integration], invite your team, and you're live. No developer
   required. [Link to 60-second setup video]
```

#### Objection 3: "What if I don't like it?"

**Answer Framework**: Risk reversal.

Remove all risk from the decision.

```
Q: What if it's not right for my team?

A: Start with a free 14-day trial. No credit card required. If it's
   not a fit, you lose nothing. If you're on a paid plan and want to
   cancel, you can do it in one click from your dashboard. No calls,
   no retention tricks.
```

#### Objection 4: "Is my data safe?"

**Answer Framework**: Certifications + specifics.

Name the exact security measures. Vague "we take security seriously" copy is worthless.

```
Q: How do you handle security?

A: We are SOC 2 Type II certified. All data is encrypted at rest
   (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3). We run on AWS/GCP with
   99.99% uptime SLA. Your data is never shared with third parties
   or used for training. [Link to security page]
```

#### Objection 5: "Will it work with my tools?"

**Answer Framework**: Integration list + API.

Show logos and name the specific tools.

```
Q: Does [Product] integrate with my existing tools?

A: Yes. We have native integrations with Slack, Jira, GitHub, Linear,
   Notion, and 40+ other tools. Connect in one click, no code required.
   For custom workflows, our REST API and webhooks handle anything else.
   [Link to integrations page]
```

### Industry-Specific Objections

Add 2-3 objections specific to the product's industry. Common patterns:

**For Developer Tools**:
- "Will it slow down my build?" -- Answer with benchmark numbers.
- "Does it work with my language/framework?" -- Answer with a compatibility matrix.
- "Can I self-host?" -- Answer with deployment options.

**For Marketing Tools**:
- "Will this actually increase conversions?" -- Answer with case study data.
- "Does it work with my CMS?" -- Answer with integration list.
- "How is this different from [Competitor]?" -- Answer with a specific comparison, not FUD.

**For Finance/HR Tools**:
- "Is it compliant with [regulation]?" -- Answer with specific certifications.
- "Can it handle our company size?" -- Answer with the largest customer example.
- "Will our accountant/lawyer/auditor accept reports from this?" -- Answer with export formats and standards.

## 8. Subheadline Rules

The subheadline is the second most important piece of copy on the page after the headline. It does the work the headline cannot do alone.

### Hero Subheadline Rules

- **Length**: 15-25 words. No shorter (too vague). No longer (nobody reads it).
- **Must answer one of these**: "How does this work?" OR "What do I get?" OR "How fast will I see results?"
- **Must include one specific number or timeframe**. This is mandatory.
- **Tone**: Calm, confident, specific. Not hype. Not excitement. Just clarity.

### Examples

**Headline**: "Stop Losing Deals to Slow Proposals"
**Subheadline**: "Generate polished proposals in 3 minutes using your CRM data. Your team closes 40% faster when proposals go out the same day."

**Headline**: "The CRM Built for Founders Who Sell"
**Subheadline**: "Set up in 5 minutes. See every deal, email, and call on one screen. Built for teams of 1-50 who need results, not configuration."

**Headline**: "AI Writes Your Changelog From Git Commits"
**Subheadline**: "Connect your repo and get a publish-ready changelog in 90 seconds. Used by 2,400 engineering teams to ship release notes without the pain."

### Subheadline Anti-Patterns (Never Do These)

- Do not repeat the headline in different words.
- Do not use the subheadline for a tagline or brand slogan.
- Do not write a subheadline that could apply to any product in the category.
- Do not start with "We" or "Our". Start with an action verb or "You/Your".

## 9. Microcopy That Converts

Microcopy is the small text around forms, buttons, toggles, and interactive elements. It is invisible when done right and devastating when done wrong.

### Form Labels

| Default (Weak) | Conversion-Optimized | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Email address | Work email | Signals B2B, filters personal emails |
| Full name | Your name | Conversational, less formal |
| Company | Company name | Slightly more specific |
| Phone number | Phone (optional) | Marking optional reduces friction |
| Password | Create a password | Action-oriented |
| Message | How can we help? | Inviting, not bureaucratic |

### Trust Hints Below CTAs

Place these directly below every CTA button. Match the hint to the CTA type.

```
Free trial CTA:
"Free 14-day trial. No credit card required. Cancel anytime."

Demo CTA:
"30-minute call. No pressure. See it live with your data."

Lead magnet CTA:
"Takes 2 minutes. We'll never spam you."

Pricing CTA:
"30-day money-back guarantee. Switch plans anytime."
```

### Pricing Toggle Labels

- BAD: "Monthly" / "Yearly"
- GOOD: "Monthly" / "Annual (save 20%)"
- BETTER: "Pay Monthly" / "Pay Annually -- save 20%"

The savings percentage must always be visible. It is the primary reason people choose annual.

### Pricing Tier Badges

Use badges to guide the visitor toward the recommended tier:

- **"Most Popular"** -- on the mid-tier plan. Social proof effect.
- **"Best Value"** -- on the annual version of any plan. Anchors the comparison.
- **"Recommended"** -- when you want to be direct about which plan fits most teams.
- **"For Teams"** or **"For Enterprise"** -- when tiers map to company size.

Never use more than one badge per pricing table. Multiple badges dilute the signal.

### Empty States and Zero-Data Copy

When a user signs up and sees an empty dashboard, the copy must guide them:

- BAD: "No data yet."
- GOOD: "You're all set. Connect your first integration to see data flowing in about 60 seconds."

### Error Messages

- BAD: "Invalid input"
- GOOD: "That email doesn't look right. Double-check the format?"

### Loading States

- BAD: "Loading..."
- GOOD: "Pulling your latest data..." or "Almost there..."

## 10. Page-Level Copy Checklist

Run this checklist before finalizing any landing page copy. Every item must be true. If any item fails, revise before outputting.

### Hero Section
- [ ] Headline is under 12 words
- [ ] Headline communicates one specific outcome
- [ ] Headline matches the visitor's awareness level
- [ ] Subheadline is 15-25 words
- [ ] Subheadline contains at least one specific number or timeframe
- [ ] Primary CTA uses approved CTA text (see Section 3)
- [ ] CTA is the highest-contrast element on the screen
- [ ] Trust hint appears below the CTA
- [ ] No banned words appear anywhere in the hero

### Social Proof
- [ ] Logo bar or metrics bar appears directly below hero
- [ ] Numbers are specific and exact (not rounded or vague)
- [ ] Testimonials include full name, title, company, and specific result
- [ ] Testimonial placement follows the rules in Section 6

### Problem Section
- [ ] Written in the customer's own language
- [ ] Uses "you/your" language, never "users/customers"
- [ ] Does not mention the product by name
- [ ] Names 2-3 specific, concrete pains
- [ ] Ends with a transition toward the solution

### Features Section
- [ ] 3-5 features maximum
- [ ] Every feature has a "so that" benefit clause
- [ ] Features map directly to the pains in the problem section
- [ ] Each feature has a visual (screenshot, GIF, or diagram)
- [ ] No banned words in any feature description

### Testimonials Section
- [ ] 2-3 testimonials with all required elements
- [ ] Each testimonial contains a specific result or metric
- [ ] Testimonials address the most common objections
- [ ] Photos and logos are included (not placeholder icons)

### Pricing Section
- [ ] 2-3 tiers clearly differentiated
- [ ] One tier is highlighted as recommended
- [ ] Annual savings percentage is visible
- [ ] CTA on each tier uses approved text
- [ ] Trust badges appear near pricing CTAs

### FAQ Section
- [ ] All 5 universal objections are addressed
- [ ] 2-3 industry-specific objections are included
- [ ] Answers follow the frameworks in Section 7
- [ ] Answers include specific numbers, timeframes, or proof points
- [ ] No answer is longer than 4 sentences

### Final CTA Section
- [ ] Headline restates the core outcome (not just "Ready to get started?")
- [ ] CTA text matches the hero CTA exactly
- [ ] Trust hint appears below the CTA
- [ ] Section is visually distinct (background color, full-width)

### Full-Page Checks
- [ ] CTA appears at least 4 times on the page
- [ ] No banned words appear anywhere on the page
- [ ] All sentences in hero copy are under 20 words
- [ ] Active voice used throughout
- [ ] "You/your" outnumbers "we/our" by at least 3:1
- [ ] Every claim is supported by a specific number or proof point
- [ ] Page reads naturally when spoken aloud (conversational tone test)
- [ ] Copy mirrors the customer's actual language (voice calibration was performed)
- [ ] Mobile viewport has been checked: CTA is visible, text is readable, no orphaned words in headlines
